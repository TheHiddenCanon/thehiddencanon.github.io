You Have Arrived

Da'ath

— the threshold you decoded —

You read it as a message. It was an address.

This is the whole argument, enacted in miniature. The cipher contained no meaning to be extracted. It contained a location — and the only way to receive it was to arrive.

This is what we believe the tradition has always transmitted: not answers, but coordinates. Not content, but the way to the threshold. The Visitor sends no messages. It leaves a door, and the means to find it.

You are among the few who crossed. If you wish to go further in — the complete private edition, and first word of Issue VI: The New Threshold — write to us, and say only: I arrived at Da'ath.
